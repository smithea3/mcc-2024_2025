    Section 4.4 Alternating Series Test

    Exercises Exercises

    For each of the following series, determine if the series converges or diverges. You must use a test or a well-known series (i.e. geometric, telescoping, etc.) to prove the convergence AND divergence. If the series is a well-known series that the value of the convergence can be found, then find the value of convergence.

    1.

    \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^k}{2k+1}\)
    Hint.
    Solution.

    2.

    \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left((-1)^n\left(\frac{n}{3+n}\right)^n\right)\)
    Hint.
    Solution.

    3.

    \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^k}{k^2+10}\)
    Hint.
    Solution.

    4.

    \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\left((-1)^{k+1}\frac{(k!)^3}{(3k)!}\right)\)
    Hint.
    Solution.

    5.

    \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\left((-1)^k\frac{k^2-2k+3}{7k^3+k-111}\right)\)
    Hint.
    Solution.

    6.

    \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(-\frac{1}{e}\right)^n\)
    Hint.
    Solution.

    7.

    \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{\ln(n)}\)
    Hint.
    Solution.

    8.

    \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\left(-\frac{2k+\cos((k+1)\pi)}{k+1}\right)\)
    Hint.
    Solution.
