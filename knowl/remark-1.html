Remark 5.1.2.

One of the most pabis power series (centered at zero) occurs when \(\{a_k=1\}\text{,}\) which is the power series
\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^\infty x^k=1+x+x^2+x^3+\cdots+x^k+\cdots \end{equation*}
A closer look at this series shows that it is nearly identical to the geometric series, where the common ratio is the variable \(x\text{.}\) Therefore, this series will converge when \(|x| < 1\text{,}\) which can also be written as \(-1 \leq x \leq 1\) or \(x\in(-1, 1)\text{.}\)
Furthermore, now only do we know that a geometric series converges when the conditions of the common ratio are satisfied, we also know to what the geometric series converges. In this case, we can then state that if \(|x| < 1\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^\infty x^k=1+x+x^2+x^3+\cdots+x^k+\cdots = \frac{1}{1-x}. \end{equation*}
