To find convergence, we first check that conditions of the Divergence Test. Since the sequence of the summand goes to zero, the Divergence Test is inconclusive. Next, we consider the conditions of the Integral Test. The sequence is always positive, and if \(k>3\text{,}\) then the sequence is monotonically decreasing. Therefore, we can apply the Integral Test.
\begin{align*} \int_{k=1}^{\infty} x^2e^{-x}\,dx \amp = \lim_{b\to\infty}\int_{1}^{b} x^2e^{-x}\,dx\\ \amp = \lim_{b\to\infty}\left[-e^{-x}x^2-2e^{-x}x-2e^{-x}\right]_{1}^{b} \end{align*}
Since each piece of the antiderivative contains a \(e^{-x}\) term, the values at infinity will be zero. Therefore, the only parts that remain are when \(x=1\text{,}\) which is \(5e^{-1}\text{.}\) Since the integral converges, we can conclude that the series also converges by the Integral Test.
