To use the Direct Comparison Test to show convergence, the "stop" relationship must be considered. Therefore, a series that is larger than the given series, but converges must be found. Since it is true that \(3k^2 < (1+2k)^2 \) for all \(k > 1\text{,}\) it is also true that \(\frac{3}{(1+2k)^2} < \frac{1}{k^2}\) and therefore, \(\displaystyle\sum_k \frac{3}{(1+2k)^2} < \sum_k\frac{1}{k^2}\text{.}\) The convergence of \(\displaystyle \sum_k\frac{1}{k^2}\) is known by the p-series test, and the relationship. So, it can be concluded that \(\displaystyle\sum_k \frac{3}{(1+2k)^2}\) also converges by the Direct Comparison Test.
