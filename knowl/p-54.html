A sequence of real numbers, which we write as \(\left\{a_k\right\}_k^\infty\text{,}\) is a function whose inputs are from the set of positive integers and their outputs are to the set of real numbers. The inputs are typically denoted with an \(k\) or an \(n\) and is called an index. The output, denoted \(a_k\) or \(a_n\) is called the \(k\)th term of the sequence.
