We can rewrite the differential equation by dividing everything by \(dx\text{,}\) which produces the new differential equation
\begin{equation*} x-y+x\frac{dy}{dx}=0 \end{equation*}
This differential equation can be written as \(xy'-y=-x\text{.}\) Dividing by \(x\) converts the differential equation to the standard form of a first-order linear differential equation:
\begin{equation*} y'-\frac{1}{x}y=-1 \end{equation*}
Using an integrating factor of \(\mu(x)=e^{\int\frac{1}{x}\,dx}\text{,}\) We get that \(\displaystyle\mu(x)=\frac{1}{x}\text{.}\)
